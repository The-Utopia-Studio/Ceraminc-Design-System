## Design Context

### Users

Internal designers, developers, and AI agents use Ceramic to build product interfaces quickly and consistently. They need human-readable guidance, stable implementation contracts, and machine-readable discovery through package exports, CLI, and MCP.

### Brand Personality

Precise, trustworthy, and composed. The Utopia Default expression is warm, confident, architectural, serious, and spare; other themes may change the visual personality without weakening the shared semantic contract.

### Aesthetic Direction

A refined product-design workbench rather than a generic component gallery. It should favor clear hierarchy, restrained ornament, border-led structure, purposeful motion, and strong typographic rhythm. Utopia Default uses its locked studio primitives; alternative themes retain their own palette, geometry, typography, icon, and motion policies.

### Design Principles

1. Make system decisions easy to discover and difficult to misuse for humans and agents alike.
2. Keep reusable component semantics stable while allowing themes to own visual and motion personality.
3. Prefer precise hierarchy and purposeful feedback over decorative effects or generic SaaS patterns.
4. Preserve accessibility, Arabic and RTL behavior, reduced motion, and keyboard use as release requirements.
5. Optimize for fast product construction without making the resulting interface feel templated or untrustworthy.
